DOG ADOPTION PROCESS

1. APPLICATION SUBMISSION

Applicants can apply for adoptions online or in person. The review takes a few days, and approval status is communicated via email or phone. Upon approval, a timeframe of 24 to 48 hours is given to complete the adoption. If an applicant applies for a pet with a pending application, they're advised to follow up after a few days, as adoptions may fall through, offering other applicants a chance.

3. MEETING THE DOG

When meeting a dog, approach calmly without direct eye contact, as it may seem aggressive. Ask the guardian for permission before interacting. Let the dog initiate contact and approach you at their pace. Be patient, allowing them to sniff you. Watch their body language for comfort cues. Respect their space and avoid sudden movements or noise. Give them time to adjust and refrain from overwhelming them with affection if they're not ready.

4. FINAL VET CHECK

The final vet check confirms the pet's health status before adoption. It includes a thorough examination by a veterinarian to detect any illnesses or underlying conditions. This check ensures the pet is physically healthy, up-to-date on vaccinations, and has received proper medical care. It assures adopters of the pet's health and well-being in its new home.

5. ADOPTION FEE

The initial costs associated with adopting a dog range from approximately 10,000 to 30,000. This includes the adoption fee, which varies widely based on factors like the dog's breed, age, and the policies of the adoption organization. Adoption fees can range from less than a lunch out to several rupees.
